OIL AND GAS INDUSTRY IN MALAYSIA

Malaysia, situated in Southeast Asia, plays a crucial role in the oil and gas industry, which is a major pillar of the nation's economy. The country began producing oil in the early 1900s from onshore fields in Sarawak and expanded into shallow-water plays in Peninsular Malaysia, Sarawak, and Sabah during the 1960s. Since then, Malaysia has ranked as the 4th largest oil producer in the Asia-Pacific region and the 2nd largest in Southeast Asia. According to Zvonimir Djerfi, President of the Asia-Pacific division, "Other locations in the Asia-Pacific region are not as central as Malaysia, and they either lack space or a capable workforce to meet the needs of the oil and gas services industry." Both short-term and long-term investments in Malaysia are likely to yield promising results.

Comparison of oil producing countries

Malaysia’s most valuable mineral resources are its reserves of petroleum and natural gas. Crude oil, refined petroleum, and, more recently, liquefied natural gas (LNG) together account for a major portion of the country’s commodity export earnings. Most of crude oil is produced in Sabah (42%), Peninsular malaysia (32%) and Sarawak (26%). These oil production helped Malaysia to stand at 4th place in oil producing countries of Asia pacific region and 2nd in South east asia after Indonesia.
